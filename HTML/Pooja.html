Narayana Bali Pooja

What is Narayana Bali Pooja?

Narayana Bali Pooja is a ritual described in Garuda Purana which is performed in all cases of unnatural death of a family member. Death can be caused by various reasons such as suicide, murder, starvation, accident, animals, arson, curse, drowning, and unusual diseases which cause untimely death.

It is also performed for those who were great sinners. There is neither ritual for cremation, nor tarpana, nor sraddha, nor asaucham for such people. Narayan Bali consists of the same rituals as a Hindu funeral but shortened to three days instead of the customary thirteen. A Brahmin priest at Trimbakeshwar, experienced in conducting these rituals, is engaged by the family performing the ritual.

Five rituals are offered to Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva, Yama, and Preta. The ritual for Brahma is not known by Brahmins outside Trimbakeshwar. A dummy body, mostly made of wheat dough, is used. Vedic Mantras are chanted to invoke the lost souls. It is believed that the ritual makes the souls of the dead possess the dummy body, which is later set alight, allowing the soul to liberate and transcend.

Why should Narayan Bali Pooja be performed in Gokarna?

Gokarna is a small town on the western coast of India, in the Kumta taluk of Uttara Kannada district, Karnataka. It is known as one of the seven important Hindu pilgrimage centers in India, with the main deity being Lord Shiva (Mahabaleshwar). Gokarna holds spiritual significance, and performing rituals such as Maha Mrityunjaya Homam there has special meaning.

Gokarna is located at the ear-shaped confluence of the two rivers Gangavali and Aghanashini. The town is revered as Bhookailasa and is often referred to as the Southern Varanasi.

It is believed that the Brahmin King Raja Mayooravarma helped bring the first families to Gokarna. The town has a history of over 1,300 years, and since the 15th century, various poojas like Narayan Bali, Tripindi Shraddha, and others have been carried out by its priests.

How to perform Narayana Bali Pooja

The following are the 10 steps to perform Narayana Bali Pooja:

  1. Narayan Bali Sankalp
  2. Pretha Sankalp
  3. Vinayaka Pooja
  4. Pretha Avahana for departed souls using Sriphala
  5. Dash Pind Pradhan
  6. Brahma, Vishnu, Rudra, Yama, Savitra Devatha Kalasha Sthapane and Avahanam
  7. Panchaka Shraddh
  8. Narayan Bali Homa
  9. Brahman Muka Dwara Panch Sukta Parayana with Prayaschita Tila Homam
  10. Dasha Daan

Benefits of the Pooja

By performing Narayana Bali Pooja, we ensure that the soul of the deceased reaches the right destination in peace. This pooja brings various community benefits related to the deceased, including improved physical health, inner peace, success in business and education, marital harmony, and the resolution of interpersonal relationships and marital barriers.

Nag Bali Pooja

The Nag Bali ritual is similar to Narayan Bali. The only difference is that instead of a dummy human body, a dummy body of a snake (made from dough) is used, but the purpose of the two rituals is completely different.

Pitrudosha Nivarana

What is Pitrudosha

Pitru Dosha refers to Dosha related to ancestors or deceased family members. It is reflected in the constellations through certain planet combinations and positions. This Dosha causes many problems in life, including family conflicts, struggles, and difficulties in almost every aspect of life, adoption of children, litigation and military issues, marriage failure, obstacles to success, decline in reputation or a series of expected problems.

Problems occurring by Pitru Dosha

  • People who suffer from Pitru Dosha must face many problems related to their children. In addition to their children, they may suffer physical or mental disabilities.
  • Couples may have problems with small things. All these problems are only because of Pitru Dosha.
  • Marriage issues; despite efforts, individuals could not get married on time.
  • A family may always be surrounded by disease, leading to physical and financial problems.
  • If a family suffers from Pitru Dosha, any member may dream of snakes or ancestors wanting food or cloth.

Tripindi Shraddha

What is Tripindi Shraddha?

Tripindi means Pinddaan of our forefathers of previous 3 generations. If anyone in the family from the previous 3 generations has passed away in very young age or old age then those people cause problems to us. To let the people free one has to do Tripindi Shraddha.

In the Hindu tradition of India, debt is recognized as three types. These are Pitru runa, Rushi runa, and Paramatma runa. If a person worships (worships) and fasts according to what our Brahmins say, he will get help from Paramatma Runa. This man got relief from Pitru Runa by doing shraddha, pitru poojan, etc. Brahmins are expected to perform various Yadnya, Shraddha, Tarpan, etc. In addition to the memory day, it can also be executed.

When and where can perform Tripindi Pooja?

In any part of the Shukla month of Shravan, Kartik, Pousha, Magh, Phalgun, Vaishakh, Tripindi Shraddha may be performed on any day of Panchami, Ashtami, Ekadashi, Trayodashi, Chaturdashi, or Amavasya. Sun usually stays in Virgo or Libra from 16 September to 15 November during drilling. Pitars are permitted to come freely onto the earth during this time. It’s therefore more advantageous to do Tripindi during this time. Only at Trimbakeshwar, which is a pilgrim of Lord Shiva, can Tripindi Shraddha.

Who can do Tripindi Shraddha?

The couples have the right to do Tripindi together. This formality can also be performed by unmarried persons. If after marriage, Hindu women go to another family, she is also not qualified to perform Tripindi or Shraddha for her parents. But for those deceased who have a relationship with her husband’s relatives, she can do Tripindi or Shraddha. When performing this procedure, you should wear a brand new unwashed white dress.

The result of Tripindi Shraddha

According to the procedure described in Shastra, Tripindi must be executed. In order to get the best results, one should have faith in God.
